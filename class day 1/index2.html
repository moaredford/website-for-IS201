    Little Bit About Me A little about me, Moa Redford; trilingual as I grew up in Norway with a Swedish mother and absorbing english though an international kindergarten, loves the outdoors as you would if you lived in the breathtaking nature of the north and an incredibly independent go getter when it comes to life and achieving what I feel is the right path for me. My story has made me who I am today. I grew up with the strongest mother you could ever imagine. She is most definitely the person in my life where I get my determination and independence from as I saw her work endless hours to provide for my brother and I. With determination comes a wild imagination of what is possible, the only person in my way is myself. Imagination that creates the vision I want for my future family, my career and also for the bucket list items I have every intent of crossing off. Some of which have been done, including going on an exchange year across the world from Norway to Mountain Green Utah for a year during high school to experience something completely new and enjoy the journey and experiences that laid beyond my comfort zone, and also of serving a mission. Serving a full time mission definitely was not always on my bucket list. However, when life was not going to my plans, it was at this point I came to understand the greater desire I had to follow the Lord's plan for me and how I did and always will trust Him and His plan more than my own. Coming from a family of converts, the journey of gaining a deep relationship with my Heavenly Father was never taught to me, it was the definition of a personal journey. A journey I never knew would be so life changing to me. Due to the family circumstances I have had, I have become a person who immensely values learning about others' journey and connecting with people who may seem as strangers at first, but to me in some cases soon become what feels like family.

    Ambisious & Leader I am the founder and owner of my own company (Redford & Co.). In my family we all make our own companies when we turn eighteen. My company and I have been hired for different services, all from home interior design, yard design, catering for large hundred people events, delivering orders of baked goods. Growing up being able to increase my mindset of how to grow as a person within my own personal potential and translating that into professional growth for my own company has been a journey or making calculated decisions. Being the owner and founder (leader) of my own I have had to start to learn more about marketing and customer service. I have had the great opportunities to be a team leader in aspects of various sports and also in youth camps. Both of which have been leadership positions where it is more focused on listening to the needs of those around me and being able to apply that to the desired outcome for the circumstances before us.

    Serving a mission is a time where you get to learn more about yourself and really dig deep to understand you and others. As I served in London I had the great learning and leading experience of being a train for a new missionary. This really involved being able to slow down the pace and meet someone where they are and at the same time being able to see where there is room and potential to challenge them to be just one percent better every day. And not only the new missionary, but also within myself. Each day, I could see where we could have done better, where they had great potential, and where I had the humbling experience to see where I lacked in various areas. Leading is not always about showing the way, it is about being a helping hand on someone else's road.
